Info About Salmon

Salmon is the common name for fish in the order of Salmoniformes. They live in the northern Atlantic and Pacific oceans, and are anadromous, which means most types of salmon are born in fresh water, migrate to the sea and return to fresh water to reproduce

Salmon appearence vaires greatly from species to species. Species like chum salmon are silvery-blue in color while some have black spots on their sides, like the Atlantic salmon Still other, like the Cherry salmon, have bright red stripes. Most of these species maintain one color when living in fresh water, then change color when they are in salt water. All information provided by Defenders of Wildlife.
